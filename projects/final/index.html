The Problem

The Bengal tigers (Panthera tigris) and villagers of Chitwan, Nepal have been in contention for decades (1979). After malaria was eliminated in the 1950s the population became a mixture of immigrants from surrounding countries and the indigenous peoples of Chitwan. Villagers became less reliant on subsistence agriculture for their source income due to increased eco-tourism in newer generations. In 1973 the Chitwan National Park was established bringing in new tourists seeking entertainment, research, work, etc. With the new eco-tourism the population has increased to 579,984 people (279,087 male and 300,897 female) in 2011.

After Chitwan National Park had established its boundaries (1999) there had been 223,260 people living within its buffer zones (an area of land designated for environmental protection). Most of the people within these zones depended on agriculture and there livestock as a source of food and income.
